The Human Simulator

 

 

The Human Simulator is an original idea designed to show you how a Turing Machine computes the result of a given input based on Alan Turing's original idea of a Turing Machine. First the hand will write your input on the tape and then begin the process of simulating, at any one time moving one cell to the left, one to the right or writing a symbol to the tape.

Here's how to get started:

  1. Either click on the Human Simulator tab of your open Turing Machine or go to the Simulate menu and select Human Computer.
  2. The human Simulator will be displayed.

  3. The labelled areas function as follows:

    A. Input Word Box - This is where you type your input for the tape. (Press INSERT to insert the blank character)
    B. Play Button - Select to start the simulation with your input word or resume a paused simulation.
    C. Pause Button - Select to pause a running simulation.
    D. Stop Button - Select to stop a running simulation
    E. Speed Buttons - Increase or decrease the speed of the simulation.
    F. Human Mind - Shows the current state the human is in.
    G. Hand - The hand that does the writing
    H. Tape - The tape to be written to and read from.

  4. Type your input word into the input box.
  5. Press play to see the simulator simulate the computation on your word.
  6. You can use Pause or Stop at any time during the computation.

NB: For non-deterministic machines the human simulator will pick a transition at random from possible transitions when it comes across states that can make multiple transitions when it reads a given symbol.
